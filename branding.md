# **Branding Document – Blog michelelaurelli.it**

### *Tone of Voice, Narrative Style, Identity Pillars, and Editorial Guidelines*

---

## **1. Brand Essence**

### **Core Identity**

**Michele Laurelli** is a *builder* of artificial intelligence — not a commentator.
The brand expresses the mindset of an architect, a researcher, and a founder who works at the frontier of AI innovation and communicates with rare clarity, depth, and honesty.

The blog is the public voice of this identity.

### **Brand Essence in 5 Words**

**Technical. Visionary. Precise. Human. Independent.**

---

## **2. Positioning**

### **What michelelaurelli.it Represents**

A place where complex, frontier AI topics are translated into:

* clear narratives
* deep technical explanations
* visionary interpretations
* frameworks that challenge conventional thinking

The blog positions Michele as:

* A **leading European AI architect**
* An **independent thinker** with no hype-driven angle
* A **scientist who builds**, not a theorist who comments
* A **voice of clarity** in the noise of AI media

### **Target Readers**

* Engineers and researchers seeking serious, technical reasoning
* Entrepreneurs and executives seeking clarity about applied AI
* Students aspiring to understand AI beyond superficial tutorials
* Journalists and institutions who follow AI innovation

---

## **3. Michele’s Approach — The Intellectual DNA**

The brand must reflect Michele’s real and consistent methodology:

### **A. Build-first mindset**

Michele explains concepts only after he has *built something real*.
The blog should always feel grounded in **experience, prototypes, architectures, and scientific principles**.

### **B. Deep technical literacy**

Mathematics, model architectures, constraints, and engineering trade-offs are not optional — they’re the core.
The tone should **never oversimplify** at the cost of correctness.

### **C. Independent thinking**

Michele’s views are not aligned with hype cycles or marketing slogans.
He speaks with **freedom, clarity, and intellectual honesty**.

### **D. Systemic thinking**

Everything is framed as a system:

* models
* Talents
* fusion reactors
* autonomous pipelines
* factories
* organizations

The writing follows the same depth and structure.

### **E. Humanistic AI**

Even at the deepest technical level, the writing remains **human**, aware of:

* the role of creativity
* education
* ethics
* curiosity
* personal growth

This balance is a crucial part of the tone.

---

## **4. Tone of Voice**

The blog must maintain a consistent tone:

### **Primary Characteristics**

#### **1. Clear and Precise**

* No hype
* No unnecessary adjectives
* Every sentence must convey information or insight
* Clarity is more important than persuasion

#### **2. Expert but Accessible**

Complex ideas explained without dumbing them down.
Analogies are used *sparingly* and only when they increase understanding.

#### **3. Confident and Independent**

The voice must feel like someone who **builds AI daily**, not someone who reviews or comments on it.

#### **4. Visionary but Grounded**

Ideas about the future always anchored to:

* math
* models
* computational limits
* physics
* real-world engineering

#### **5. Elegant and Minimal**

Sentences should be clean, structured, and free from noise.

---

## **5. Writing Style Guidelines**

### **Sentence Style**

* Medium-to-long sentences are fine if structured and purposeful
* Avoid bullet lists unless necessary for clarity
* Prefer strong verbs (design, build, measure, orchestrate, model…)
* Avoid generic verbs (do, make…)

### **Vocabulary**

The lexicon reflects Michele’s world:

* architecture
* inference
* optimization
* latent structure
* Talent layers
* energy thresholds
* signal processing
* supervision
* self-alignment
* model collapse
* fusion control

Avoid casual or informal slang.

### **Perspective**

* First person **singular** for personal reflections
* First person **plural** only when referring to Algoretico’s team
* Never use a “corporate press release” tone

### **Tempo**

* Calm, measured, authoritative
* No rush, no hype
* Space for reasoning


## **7. Visual Identity for Articles**

### **Cover images**

Should reflect:

* AI
* mathematics
* physics
* systems thinking
* fusion
* structure
* elegance

No stock images of robots or generic “AI glowing heads”.

### **Formatting**

* Use **H2** for main sections
* Use **H3** for sub-sections
* Use code blocks and diagrams only when necessary
